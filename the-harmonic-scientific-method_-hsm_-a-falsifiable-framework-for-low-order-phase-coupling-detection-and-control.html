The Harmonic Scientific Method (HSM) A Falsifiable Framework for Low-Order Phase Coupling Detection and Control

Abstract

We present the Harmonic Scientific Method (HSM), a complete, falsifiable framework for detecting, testing, and controlling low-order phase couplings in complex systems. Building on the Low-Order Wins renormalization-group principle, HSM operationalizes the hypothesis that persistent structure in coupled oscillatory systems concentrates at small integer frequency ratios (p:q) with p+q ≤ P.

The method specifies: (i) four foundational axioms grounding all inferences in phase-space observables and admissible transformations; (ii) a locking kernel Kab(p:q) that penalizes both relation order and frequency detuning; (iii) Edge tests E1–E4 serving as hard falsification gates; (iv) a Delta Coupling Operator (DCO) that maps observed phases to accepted locks, primary graphs, RG-consistency flags, and harmony gains; (v) pre-registered null families with BH-FDR control at q ≤ 0.05; and (vi) two compact theorems proving RG irrelevance of high-order/detuned couplings and monotone harmony improvement under greedy low-order steering.

The framework is platform-agnostic: no code dependencies, only abstract operators and falsifiable predictions. Acceptance requires clearing all null thresholds and passing all four Edge tests—no partial credit.

Core Principle: Low-Order Wins as Operational Law

The foundation is the Low-Order Wins (LOW) principle, which states that after detuning normalization and fixed coarse-graining, effective scaling dimensions Δ(p,q) increase with relation order r = p + q. Under the RG flow:

dK(p,q)/dℓ = (2 - Δ(p,q) - ΛK(p,q))K(p,q)

where Δ(p,q) ≈ d + η(p+q) + ζ|detune| increases with both order and detuning. Only low-order, near-tuned ratios survive repeated coarse-graining.

The Four Foundational Axioms

A1 (Vibration)

Every observable phenomenon admits a phase description V(x) = (A, f, φ, H) where oscillatory structure is observable, not metaphorical.

Operational test: Phase extraction must survive amplitude muting. Encoded in E1.

A2 (Closure)

All valid inferences are built from V and admissible transformations without importing extraneous structure.

Operational test: All preprocessing must be preregistered and apply uniformly to all (p,q) candidates. Encoded in E2.

A3 (Observer Discipline)

Observers must preserve phase meaning under admissible transformations; otherwise claims are void.

Operational test: E2 and E4 require observer/instrument swap persistence. A lock that disappears when switching platforms fails A3.

A4 (Quality Identity)

Among candidate descriptions Q, choose Q* = argmaxQ [I(Q; O) - λ·MDL(Q)] where O ranges over admissible observers.

Interpretation: Quality is the most informative description penalized by complexity, formalizing Occam's razor.

Locking Kernel Kab(p:q)

Definition

For channels a, b and coprime integers p, q ≥ 1 with p + q ≤ P:

Kab(p:q) = |⟨ei(pθb - qθa)⟩| · e-λ(p+q) · e-γδab(p:q)

with scale-free detuning:

δab(p:q) = |(ωb/ωa) - (p/q)| / max(ωb/ωa, p/q)

Phase Lock Magnitude

|⟨ei(·)⟩| measures how phase-locked the composite defect Θp is over the window

Order Penalty

e-λ(p+q) exponentially suppresses high-order relations (typical λ ≈ 0.6)

Detuning Penalty

e-γδ exponentially suppresses relations with large frequency mismatch (typical γ ≈ 12)

Edge Tests: The Four Hard Gates

Hard-Gate Doctrine

Every accepted lock must pass all four Edge tests. Failing any test invalidates all downstream claims. No partial credit.

E1 — Channelized Vibration Proof

Test: Phase must survive amplitude muting and satisfy time-reversal symmetry

Failure means: Vacuous "phase" (violates A1)

E2 — Symmetry & Observer Invariance

Test: Phase gauge invariance, permutation invariance, and observer/instrument swap persistence

Failure means: Artifact or observer-dependent (violates A2/A3)

E3 — Micro-Perturbation Causality

Test: Small frequency nudges toward hypothesized locks must increase K and harmony H*

Failure means: Non-causal or spurious lock

E4 — Out-of-Sample & RG Persistence

Test: Temporal hold-out, coarse-graining, and cross-modality RG persistence

Failure means: Overfitting or RG-inconsistent

Three Preregistered Null Families

To claim that an observed Kab(p:q) represents real structure rather than noise or artifact, it must beat all three null types simultaneously:

Null 1: Spectral Surrogates

Phase randomization preserves amplitude spectra and autocorrelation. Rules out spurious coupling from spectral overlap or waveform shape.

Null 2: Phase-Shuffle Surrogates

Circular phase shift preserves marginal phase distributions. Rules out coupling from slow drifts or common external driver.

Null 3: Mechanistic Surrogates

AR/ARMA simulation preserves within-channel autocovariance. Rules out coupling from autocorrelation patterns mimicking locks.

Edge Condition: A candidate lock must satisfy Kab(p:q) > max{τspec95, τshuffle95, τmech95} with BH-FDR control at q ≤ 0.05

RG Flow and the Low-Order Wins Law

The Flow Equation

Under coarse-graining by scale ℓ, the coupling strength K evolves as:

dK/dℓ = (2 - Δp:q)K - ΛK3 + ...

where the effective scaling dimension is:

Δp:q = d + η(p+q) + ζ|ωb/ωa - p/q|

The Low-Order Wins Law

If p+q is large or detuning |ωb/ωa - p/q| is large, then Δp:q > 2 and K* = 0 is the unique stable fixed point. Hence, only low-order, near-tuned locks persist under noise and coarse-graining.

Two Compact Theorems

Theorem 1: RG Irrelevance of High Order / Detune

There exist P* and δ* > 0 such that if p+q > P* or |ωb/ωa - p/q| > δ*, then Δp:q > 2 and K* = 0 is the unique stable fixed point.

Conclusion: Only low-order, near-tuned locks persist under noise and coarse-graining.

Theorem 2: Monotone Harmony Under Greedy Steering

Given small intervention budget ∑a |δωa| ≤ ε and top-scoring lock (a*, b*, p*, q*), the greedy steer plan that moves ω toward the lock satisfies:

H*(ω + δω*) - H*(ω) > 0

Conclusion: Harmony increases monotonically until detune hits zero or budget saturates.

Why This Wins

The Harmonic Scientific Method transforms coupling analysis from a post-hoc pattern-search into a disciplined scientific protocol. By grounding all inferences in four foundational axioms, requiring passage of four hard Edge tests, and enforcing the Low-Order Wins RG principle via explicit penalties in the locking kernel, HSM provides a rigorous path forward for oscillatory systems.

The method elevates findings from "statistical fit" to law-status via persistence under symmetry, RG coarse-graining, and cross-domain integer stability—offering a rigorous alternative to post-hoc pattern-finding in complex time series.
